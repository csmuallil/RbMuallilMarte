Information

Similar to her debut album, 21's main sound was heavily inspired by Motown soul but was now further influenced by country and southern blues music due to Adele's visit to the US. Pop and disco can also be heard in some songs. The ballad and torch filled album was inspired by the fallout of her relationship with her ex-lover and the what she felt due to it, with most songs starting off as "drunken diary ramblings" and were later refined into musical compositions.

The album helped Adele improve her songwriting skills and helped her to develop her vocal confidence and face her emotions with help from her collaboraters. The singer used the album to express her emotions regarding her relationship and her ex-partner which helped her feel free; we can see this in its themes of anger and bitterness to feelings of loneliness, regret and finally... acceptance.
