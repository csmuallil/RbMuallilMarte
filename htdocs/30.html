Information

After a six-year hiatus, this new era of Adele featured genres such as Pop, Soul, and Jazz. Hailed by critics as her most candid and powerful work to date, Adele herself calls the album sensitive as she opens up more through her touching lyrics and moving melodies. The album itself follows almost a chronological chain of events from Adele reflecting on her recent divorce and past mistakes, going through feelings of depression, to finally coming to terms with herself, her past mistakes, and finally being ready to love again.

Following tough times in Adele's life, "30" mainly speaks on her divorce but also includes songs on motherhood, depression, loneliness, and dating. She writes about her ex-partner, dating in L.A., her relationship with her son, and the healing process post-divorce with the help of her friends. After writing the album, she said that it really helped her heal, learn a lot about herself, and discover useful mentalities to live with. Adele, in an interview, said she'd never felt more peaceful in her life.
